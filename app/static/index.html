Mobility in brussels
An interactive comparison tool

Explore the STIB network flow of the past few months and relate it to the stops density and major events in Brussels.

Map view

This map present 3 layers: a base map (topology), the geographical STIB stops density (colored area), and the STIB flow (colored lines on top). You can change the displayed layers using the upper-right button. You can also navigate the map as in other traditional map-based applications.

Below the map, a two-sided datetime range picker allows you to explore different time windows. When the datetime range changes, the STIB flow layer changes accordingly

Area : Stops density

The density of STIB stops in Brussels has been computed using a gaussian kernel density estimation. Low densities (places where there are not many, or not any stops around) are red, average densities in blue and high densities (places with lots of STIB stops around) in yellow.

Line : Vehicle speed

The color of the line indicates how fast a vehicle goes from a stop to another, in average in the selected time frame. Note that the speed is computed in straight line between 2 stops.

The color scale goes from red: 15min or more to ride 1km to green: 0min to ride 1km

The thickness of the lines indicate how many vehicles per hour in avergae passed by this section (again, in the selected time frame)

Detailled informations

If you click on a line, a popup opens and displays detailled information about a section between 2 stops.
